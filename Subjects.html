Astronomy

Astronomy is a core class and subject taught at Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. It is the branch of magic and science that studies stars and the movement of planets. Students use their wands with an Orrery to predict the next eclipse, the only known practical form of magic used in the class. Astronomy lessons are spent learning the names of stars and the movements of planets. It is a required class from year one until year five, and becomes an optional subject for the final two years.

Charms

Charms is a core class and subject taught at Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. Unsurprisingly, it specializes in the teaching of charms. Even though mastering charm-work is clearly essential to performing the greater part of magic, charm-work is seen as a "softer option" by some. Charms is a required subject for all students in their first five years at Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. The spells learned in Charms class are taken from textbooks. Students are taught specific wand movements and proper pronunciation. Often, students partner up in class to experiment on one another.

Defense against the Dark Arts

Defence Against the Dark Arts (abbreviated as DADA) is a subject taught at Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry and Ilvermorny School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. In this class, students study and learn how to defend themselves against all aspects of the Dark Arts, including dark creatures, curses, hexes and jinxes (dark charms), and dueling. Defence Against the Dark Arts is a required subject from first year to fifth year. The curriculum of the class varies greatly depending on what the professor at the time deems appropriate.

Flying

Flying, also known as Broom Flight Class, is a subject taught at Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. It is taught by Madam Rolanda Hooch, the Hogwarts Flying Instructor and Quidditch referee. The subject teaches students how to fly broomsticks. The course aims of these lessons are largely aimed towards providing students with a basic understanding of the only the most elementary principles behind the study of broomology, which is to say, both how to handle a broom and progressively work on various techniques and maneuvers performed while in flight, as well as receiving proper instructions on the subject of proper broom care and maintenance.

Herbology

Herbology is the study of magical and mundane plants and fungi, making it the wizarding equivalent to botany. Herbology is a core class and subject taught at Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. In this class, students learn to care for and utilize plants, learn about their magical properties and what they are used for. Many plants provide ingredients for potions and medicine, while others have magical effects of their own right. Herbology is a mandatory class at Hogwarts for the first five years of a student's education. Students spend class time learning about the different varieties of magical plants that exist. The further into a student's education, the more difficult and dangerous the plants become.

History of Magic

History of Magic is a core class and subject taught at Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. The class involves studying magical history and doesn't require the use of magic. From the first year to the fifth, students learn about the 'History of Wizards and the Magical World,' with a focus on remembering dates, names, and events. The class is completed with an O.W.L. exam that only has a written section.

Potions

Potions is a core class and subject taught at Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. In this class, students learn how to brew potions using specific recipes and various magical ingredients, starting with simple ones and progressing to more advanced ones. Potions is a required subject for students from the first year to the fifth year. While potion recipes can be found in books, the intricacies of timing, ageing, stirring techniques, and bottling are more difficult to learn without the guidance of experienced masters.

Transfiguration

Transfiguration is a core class and subject taught at Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. It involves learning the art of changing the form and appearance of an object or person, commonly known as "Transfiguration." The magic is considered complex and dangerous, with limits governed by Gamp's Law of Elemental Transfiguration. There are many branches of Transfiguration, including Trans-Species Transformation and Human Transfiguration. It's regarded as "very hard work" and "more scientific" than charms-work. Transfiguration is required for all first-year to fifth-year students, with an option for a N.E.W.T. course in the sixth and seventh year.
